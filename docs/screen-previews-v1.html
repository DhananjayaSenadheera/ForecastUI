WHY IT LOOKS LIKE THIS

  • Bold central estimate inside the range — a bare min–max misleads (users fixate on extremes); the marked dot fixes that.
  • Natural frequencies, not percentages: "7 of 10 similar seasons" with an icon-array pictograph — the best-evidenced chance encoding for low-numeracy users.
  • Confidence = icon + label + plain reason, mapped from the API's frozen Low/Medium/High + Reason.
  • Factor breakdown and chart are collapsed one tap below — depth for Tharindu without cluttering Sunil's answer.

WHY IT LOOKS LIKE THIS

  • Same layout as the high-confidence screen — honesty is expressed through content, not a scary different design.
  • "Rough price estimate" + honest reason replaces vague hedging — blanket disclaimers destroy trust; specific reasons build it.
  • LowTrust=true renders the amber data-age banner; the wide band is shown, never a fake precise number.
  • This is the screen 63 of 82 crops will show today, so it must feel first-class, not like an error page.

What to check before signing the PRD

Redline anything here and I'll revise the PRD before FE-0 wireframes begin.

  1. Brand feel — does the teal read as trustworthy and neutral to you? (It was chosen because green/blue/red/maroon are all party-owned.)
  2. The signature screen (3) — hero number, range with marked centre, "7 of 10 seasons" phrasing: is this how you want uncertainty spoken to farmers?
  3. The honesty screen (5) — comfortable shipping a "rough estimate" framing for the 63 fallback crops, with the data-age warning visible?
  4. Sinhala drafts — copy is my draft, flagged for native-speaker review; tab names (මුල / වගාව / මගේ බෝගය / මිල) especially.
  5. No registration wall — screens assume anonymous browsing, which requires your API-6 decision (anonymous read vs guest account).
